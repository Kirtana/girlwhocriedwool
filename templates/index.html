Girl wields pokey sticks other than a pencil

The Girl Who Cried Wool has its roots in a small family venture - adventure might be more appropriate - that started with a small shop called 'The Waxery,' in 1994, which, unfortunately, is no more. It began in Madras, India, where my cousins, 2 aunts, my mother and I made candles, quilts, stuffed dolls, cushion covers with mirrors - just about anything we could craft we tried. When I moved back to the U.S. in 1998, having been alternately teased and admired for knitting or crocheting in a small comfy nook in front of the tv at school between classes, I took it upon myself - well mostly upon my mother, actually - to begin my adventure again, online with a web presence called 'Sanathana Exclusives.' When I started to travel again, I could not dedicate the time and energy to maintaining my site and had to close it down. As time passed, I began, again, to drive those around me crazy with my latent need for crafting till I could not help but cry 'WOOL!' and here we are now... Girl Who Cried Wool found an online presence in 2008 - the somewhat defunct google site can be found here. Do have fun exploring my little site :)
